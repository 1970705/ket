# Make Atoll Vocabulary Design

**Island**: Make Atoll
**Theme**: Making, creating, and doing actions
**Word Count**: 30 words (6 words per level × 5 levels)
**KET Level**: A2 Key English Test vocabulary
**Document Version**: 1.0

---

## Overview

Make Atoll focuses on action verbs related to creation, production, and accomplishment. These are essential KET vocabulary words that describe things students do every day.

**Learning Objectives**:
1. Master basic action verbs for daily activities
2. Understand the difference between similar action words (make vs. create, start vs. begin)
3. Learn vocabulary for school and home activities
4. Build confidence in describing what they can do

## Word Families and Related Words

### make family
- make → maker (noun) → making (gerund)
- Related: create, build, produce, manufacture

### do family
- do → doing → did → done (irregular)
- Related: action, activity

### learn family
- learn → learner → learning
- Related: study, practice, know

### create family
- create → creative (adjective) → creativity (noun) → creation (noun)
- Related: make, invent, design

### work family
- work → worker → working
- Related: job, employment, effort

## Common Confusions and Tips

### make vs. do
- **make**: Use when creating something new (make a cake, make a bed)
- **do**: Use for actions and tasks (do homework, do exercise)

### start vs. begin
- Same meaning, interchangeable
- "Start" is more common in everyday speech
- "Begin" is slightly more formal

### draw vs. paint
- **draw**: Using pencils, pens, chalk (lines)
- **paint**: Using paint and brushes (colors)

### learn vs. study
- **study**: The effort you put in (reading, practicing)
- **learn**: The result of studying (gaining knowledge)

### read vs. write
- Opposite actions - input vs. output
- Good to teach together as pairs

## Grammar Patterns

### Present Simple (Daily Activities)
- I **make** breakfast every morning.
- She **reads** books at night.
- They **work** in a factory.

### Imperatives (Instructions)
- **Clean** your room!
- **Draw** a picture.
- **Use** a pencil.

### Can for Ability
- I can **swim**.
- Can you **play** football?
- She can **cook** well.

### -ing Form (Continuous)
- I am **reading** now.
- She is **making** a cake.
- They are **studying** English.

## Teaching Activities

### Level 1-2 Activities
1. **Simon Says**: Use action verbs ("Simon says... draw a cat")
2. **Mime Game**: Students act out verbs for others to guess
3. **Picture Cards**: Match verbs to pictures
4. **Daily Routine**: Describe what they do every day

### Level 3-4 Activities
1. **Story Sequencing**: Use start/continue/finish to order a story
2. **Project Planning**: Plan something they want to make
3. **Interview**: Ask classmates what they can do
4. **Instructions**: Write simple how-to guides

### Level 5 Activities
1. **Invention Convention**: Design and present an invention
2. **Factory Tour**: Describe how things are made
3. **Career Discussion**: Talk about different jobs
4. **Organization Challenge**: Organize a messy space

## Assessment Criteria

### Recognition (Reading)
- Student can identify the word when shown
- Student can match word to picture
- Student understands meaning in context

### Production (Writing/Spelling)
- Student can spell the word correctly
- Student can use the word in a simple sentence
- Student knows the past tense form (for irregular verbs)

### Application (Speaking)
- Student can pronounce the word clearly
- Student can use the word in appropriate context
- Student can respond to instructions using these verbs
